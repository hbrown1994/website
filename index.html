About

Hunter Brown is a composer, improviser, electronic musician, and audio engineer based in Chicago and Berlin. His experimental practice explores sonic perception, representation, materiality, and abstraction through works spanning fixed-media electronic music, mixed-media electroacoustic compositions, live-electronic music, sound installations, and interdisciplinary performances. Hunter's technologically focused approach involves custom software systems that push the physical limits of digital technology through bespoke analysis and synthesis algorithms, machine learning and artificial intelligence, machine listening, psychoacoustics, and spatial audio. His recent work investigates the latent spaces between the unstable material properties of digital audio systems, standardized machine listening protocols, and human sonic perception.

Hunter’s work has been presented internationally at festivals, conferences, universities, and venues such as Big Ears (Knoxville), Conference on AI Music Creativity (Graz), Darmstädter Ferienkurse (Darmstadt), Experimental Sound Studio (Chicago), Festival Nueva Ópera (Buenos Aires), International Computer Music Conference (Daegu), International Workshop on Multilayer Music Representation and Processing (Milan), IRCAM Manifeste (Paris), New Interfaces for Musical Expression (Porto Alegre), and Petersberg Arts Space (Berlin).

Hunter is a freelance audio engineer specializing in contemporary classical, jazz, improvised, and electroacoustic music. He also serves as technical director for Ensemble Dal Niente and co-runs the record label Party Perfect!!! alongside collaborator Dominic Coles. He curates and organizes Bloc Sound, a series for music and sound art from the aesthetic fringes of experimental music. He has also taught electronic music production and composition at the Oberlin Conservatory of Music and the University of Chicago.
